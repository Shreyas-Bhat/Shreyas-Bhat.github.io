I am a second-year Ph.D. student in the Computer Science Department at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, advised by Prof. Junier Oliva. My research interests broadly span statistical reasoning over structured data, interactive machine learning, intelligent and efficient information acquisition, and model-based reinforcement learning—particularly using world models and reinforcement learning to solve planning problems.

This summer, I am interning at Adobe, where I am working on causal inference to improve data-driven customer journey and analytics.

Previously, I was a Research Intern at QTIM, collaborating with researchers at Harvard University, MIT, and Massachusetts General Hospital. There, I developed deep learning models to predict the gene expression of oncologic drivers, advised by Prof. Jayashree Kalpathy-Cramer, Prof. Bruce Rosen, Prof. Albert Kim, and Prof. Christopher Bridge.

As an undergraduate at BITS Pilani, Goa, I was affiliated with APPCAIR AI Labs. I worked with Prof. Ashwin Srinivasan on scientific discovery using constraint optimization with large language models, as well as reliable model compression.
