I am a second-year PhD Student in the Computer Science Department at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I am interested in Large Language Models for Decision Making, Reinforcement Learning, Active Learning, and AI for Health. I am focusing on a) Developing the Universal Neural Inference Machine, a foundational model for tabular and set-structured data that performs cross-dataset generalization and active feature acquisition. b) Interactive-ML and c) enhancing the performance of black-box decision-makers (LLMs) using ideas from Active Learning and Reinforcement Learning.

  • Generative Models: Foundational models for tabular data, images, languages, sets, etc.
  • Interactive-ML: modelling intelligent interactions between agents that cooperate or contradict (can be extended to human-agent interactions).
  • Model-based Reinforcement Learning: leverage world-models and RL for planning problems.
  • Large Foundation Models: train and evaluate large language models and multi-modal models.
  • Active Feature Acquisition: acquire the most informative features for cost-sensitive problems.

Previously, I was a Research Intern at QTIM, Harvard University/MIT and MGH, where I developed deep learning models to predict gene expression of oncologic drivers of brain metastases from multi-sequence MRI, working with Prof. Jayashree Kalpathy-Cramer, Prof. Bruce Rosen, Prof. Albert Kim and Prof. Christopher Bridge.

As an undergraduate student at BITS Pilani Goa, I was affiliated with APPCAIR AI Labs where I worked with Prof. Ashwin Srinivasan on generating drug-like molecules for specific targets using Large Language Models and worked on reliable model compression. I also had the opportunity to work with Prof. Narendra Ahuja at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign on plant phenotype prediction.
